# Card Deck Exercise — Masking Strategy Selection

**Thursday Masking Day · Tilburg Multiscale Summerschool 2026**
**Duration:** 30 minutes · **Groups:** 3–4 participants · **Format:** Facilitated group activity

---

## Overview

Two SYNAPSIS card decks are used in combination:

| Deck | Cards | Purpose |
|------|-------|---------|
| **Data Tarot** | 35 concept cards | Introduce a masking jargon term through metaphor, prompt, and ice-breaker |
| **Masking Lab Deck** | 30 reflection cards (Past / Present / Future) | Surface tensions and open questions around a masking decision |

Together, the decks move participants from *understanding concepts* (tarot) to *sitting with real tensions* (lab deck). A group might land on "The Phantom" (anonymisation) and then draw "The Re-Identifier" from the Future suit — that juxtaposition is the exercise.

## Instructions for participants

### Step 1 — Draw from the Data Tarot (1 card per group)

Each group draws **one card at random** from the AV-masking cluster of the Data Tarot:

> *If using printed cards, shuffle the AV cluster face-down and draw one.*
> *If using the digital deck, use the "Draw Random" button on the web version.*

**AV-masking cluster (recommended draw pool):**

| Card | Jargon | Key question |
|------|--------|-------------|
| **I. The Mask** | Data Masking | What would happen to your research if participants could never be re-identified? |
| **II. The Guardian** | GDPR / Privacy | Who decides what "the right reason" is for accessing your research data? |
| **XI. The Eye** | Audiovisual Data | What does a video of your research show that a transcript never could? |
| **XII. The Steward** | Data Stewardship | What's the difference between owning data and being responsible for it? |
| **XIII. The Phantom** | Anonymisation | Is truly anonymous data still useful? Where's the line between protection and destruction? |
| **Wild — The Withdrawal** | Scenario | A participant withdraws consent after data collection. You have 3 months of video. What now? |
| **Wild — The Shortcut** | Scenario | You need to share a dataset this week. Full masking takes three days. What do you cut? |

Read the card's **metaphor** aloud. Do the **ice-breaker** if time allows.

---

### Step 2 — Draw from the Masking Lab Deck (3 cards, one per suit)

Each group draws **one card per suit** — Past, Present, Future — from the Masking Lab Deck:

> *Shuffle each suit separately and draw one face-down.*

Then place the three cards alongside the Data Tarot card on the table.

**Selected cards for MDIG (pre-sorted by suit):**

**PAST** — *What you inherited*

| Card | Reading |
|------|---------|
| **II. The Index Card** | A consent form, photocopied, folded, faded. What was agreed once on paper, governing what is asked of the data today. |
| **VI. The Unnamed Face** | *Whose face do you carry that never gave you their name?* |
| **VII. The Protocol** | *What did your ethics protocol assume about the world — that is no longer true?* |
| **X. Loss** | What we cannot recover. The tape that was wiped, the consent never sought, the participants who are gone. |

**PRESENT** — *The active situation*

| Card | Reading |
|------|---------|
| **XI. The Mask** | What you wear so others can be themselves. The kind of mask says what you protected and what you preserved. |
| **XVI. What Stays** | *If you mask a face, what stays? The expression, the gesture, the gaze, the silence?* |
| **XIX. Trust** | The currency that masking spends. Subjects trust the researcher. Researchers trust the toolchain. |
| **XX. Friction** | Every extra step that pushes the researcher toward "I'll just keep the raw video on my laptop." |

**FUTURE** — *The open questions*

| Card | Reading |
|------|---------|
| **XXIV. The Re-Identifier** | The future adversary who undoes what we did. The card you do not want to draw — but must keep in the deck. |
| **XXVI. The Next Steward** | *Who teaches the next generation of data stewards — and what does the curriculum say about masking?* |
| **XXVII. The Unseen Training Set** | *What would you let a model train on if you couldn't see who was in it?* |
| **XXIX. Sustainability** | Who runs the toolchain in year five? Who pays the GPU bill? Who answers the email? |

### Step 3 — Fill in the four corners

Using your four cards (1 tarot + 3 lab deck), fill in this grid **for your own research dataset:**

```
┌─────────────────────────┬─────────────────────────┐
│       PARADOX           │       CHALLENGE          │
│                         │                          │
│  A tension the cards    │  A concrete problem your │
│  surfaced that has no   │  group faces in masking  │
│  easy resolution.       │  your data.              │
│                         │                          │
├─────────────────────────┼─────────────────────────┤
│      OPPORTUNITY        │       QUESTION           │
│                         │                          │
│  Something the cards    │  One question you still  │
│  suggest you could do   │  don't know the answer   │
│  differently or better. │  to after today.         │
│                         │                          │
└─────────────────────────┴─────────────────────────┘
```

*Tip: the paradox often lives between the tarot card and the Future suit card. The challenge usually comes from the Present suit card.*

---

### Step 4 — Share with the room

Each group reads out:
- Which four cards they drew (10 seconds)
- Their **paradox** (30 seconds)
- Their **question** (10 seconds)

No need to have answers — the questions are the output.

## Facilitator notes

**Before the session:**
- Print or sort both decks in advance.
- Pre-separate the Masking Lab Deck into three face-down piles (Past / Present / Future).
- For the Data Tarot: either print the AV cluster (7 cards listed above) or open the web version on a shared screen.

**During group work:**
- Circulate and listen — groups often get stuck on "what does this card mean for my data?" Push them: "Pick the tension that surprises you most."
- If a group finishes early: ask them to pick a **second** Past card and see if it changes their paradox.

**Common pitfalls:**
- Groups try to resolve the paradox rather than sit with it — remind them the grid has a "question" corner, not an "answer" corner.
- Students who don't have their own video data can use the **triadic concept generation dataset** as their scenario.
