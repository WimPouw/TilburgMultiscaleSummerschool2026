These 4 groups span the full spectrum — incremental → radical. Ideal for demonstrating masking trade-offs.

Why this dataset for masking?

  • Multi-person — 3 participants, each with a dedicated camera stream
  • Gesture-rich — sketching, pointing, enacting — throughout
  • Multi-camera — tests masking across different angles and distances
  • Known ground truth — transcripts + VCode coding → MaskBench can verify pose
The overhead camera is a bonus challenge: no faces but gestures and handwriting on sketches are visible — what should you mask?

The privacy–utility trade-off

Must remove

  • Face, hair, clothing
  • Voice (microphones)
  • Handwriting on sketches
  • Background cues (Stanford lab)

Must preserve

  • Body pose & gesture trajectory
  • Hand movements (pointing, sketching)
  • Shared artefacts on the table
  • Temporal sync across 4 streams
